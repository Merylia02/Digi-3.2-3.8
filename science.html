12Earth & Space

  • This course is Year 12 Earth and Space Science.
  • By taking this course you will be mainly investigating and learning information about geography, the ocean system, the atmosphere system, and astronomy
  • Future course opportunities this course provides are 13ESSC.

Advice from students


Anonymous no.2

In-class notes are very important!!! Work on diagrams because they CAN contribute to a higher grade if you don't write a good answer. Technically you do not have to do the achieved sections but it is much safer to do them! They don't take very long, usually a labeling diagram or filling out table questions. For the internals, make sure to check the marking schedule and past exemplars for the format your reports should be in so you don't miss anything.

12Physics

  • This course is Year 12 Physics.
  • By taking this course you will be mainly investigating and learning to understand different aspects of physics; This includes mechanics, electricity & electromagnetism, & more.
  • Future course opportunities this course provides are 13PHYS.

Advice from students


Anonymous no.16

Study using 'No Brain Too Small' and the exam past papers (the extended catalog can be found on the 'No Brain Too Small' website). Make sure you know all the concepts and memorize the definitions/typical written answers that come up nearly every year (e.g. centripetal force). Always round to the lowest number of significant figures that are given in the question, and never forget units. In the exams, a formula will usually only be used once. Some 'explain' questions can be answered by just putting in the relevant equation and explaining it. Do any homework assigned throughout the year so that you don't have to do all the study at once.

Georgia

Give yourself plenty of time for internals as biology internals are quite long. Whether it is an at-home internal - don’t procrastinate just get it done! Or if it’s an in-class internal - make sure you have soaked up all the knowledge possible so you can write fast so you don’t miss out on anything. Always listen to the lessons thoroughly and take as many notes as possible. For exams, it is SUPER helpful to make mind maps of everything in the topic on one piece of paper. I did this and read it right before I walked into my exams and remembered everything.
