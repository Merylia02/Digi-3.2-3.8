Anonymous no.16

Work through ALL the exercises in the Walker maths book (or whichever booklet is used during class time / assigned for homework) and try to avoid looking at the answers until you've finished a question. The DnD books (with the practice papers) are great for study, as well as the past practice exam papers-- try and do as many as possible and pick up on patterns. Don't underestimate your abilities-- sometimes just coming back to a question after 10 minutes will help you understand it

  • This course is Year 12 Mathematics Extension / Enrichment.
  • By taking this course you will be learning more difficult mathematic skills some of which will be above your level
  • Future course opportunities this course provides are 13CALX, 13MATS, & 13CALC.

Advice from students

Anonymous no.1

Take practice tests as the practice tests are the most beneficial to a student's understanding of match and how the test environment may feel to them. Read the question carefully as they often hide the information between the words and not on the diagram they provide. It is also important that they are careful with the test and read the questions carefully as most people lose marks not because they didn’t have the knowledge required for it but because they accidentally miss read the questions. You also must show your work in an orderly manner otherwise you might be marked down for it.
