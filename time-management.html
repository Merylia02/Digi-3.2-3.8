Time management methods



Kanban project management method


This is my personal favorite management method that can be applied to a variety of things. I have used this management method in subjects like (Business, Photography, Digital Technologies, and more). This method requires you to create a board with 3 categories (To do, Doing, Done) you can add more if required. You basically list out what you need to do into the 'To Do' category and then move it to 'Doing' when you're working on it; When you are done with the task you can then move it to 'Done'. It's as simple as that. Online tools that can help you set up your board it Microsoft Planner, Trello, and more that you can look for.


Calendar


You probably know that it is very important to have a calendar when keeping track of your work; What you might not know is the difference between a digital calendar and a physical one. Digital calendars are great, they are able to have many features attached to them. it also has its own cons that is the fact that it's difficult to visualise how much time you have left on large projects, nor do you get the satisfaction of going through a day. I highly recommend printing a calendar at the start of the year.


Managing group


It is also recommended that you and your friends tell each other what you have to do. This helps each other keep track of what work you have next and strengthens relationships. You can even make your own rules on how you help each other.


Priority Matrix


The priority matrix is a time management method focusing on getting things done based on their priority. Things that are urgent and important are the first priority, then comes important, then urgent. This makes sure that you aren't missing out on something important over something that isn't.
